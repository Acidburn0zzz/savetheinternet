What Is Going On In The EU

Last year, thousands of citizens took action to Save the Internet during the fight for net neutrality in the European Parliament. Supported by this campaign, more than 40,000 faxes, thousands of emails, and hundreds of phone calls were made and together, we convinced politicians to defend our fundamental rights online.

But the fight isn’t over. Even now, the Council of the European Union is debating about whether they should support the Parliament’s strong call for net neutrality - or open the door to a tiered internet, which would be devastating to the long-term welfare of the telecoms market, competition, innovation, and human rights online.

The Italian Presidency of the Council has the opportunity to deliver the promise of net neutrality to EU citizens during his term, but he is being pressured by members states like France to delay the proposal. Other nations, such as the UK and Denmark, are pushing for the inclusion of harmful provisions which would enable discrimination on the network - effectively neutering the good work of the Parliament. Some, like Spain, are pushing for nationalistic provisions which would service the economic interests of their local telcos. Finally, a large group of countries like Germany and Sweden are absent from the debate altogether; by refusing to acknowledge the global situation of the EU market, these nations decrease the likelihood that the Italian Presidency will be able to reach an agreement on TSM before the end of the year.

These states are undermining the promise of the EU Parliament, and are threatening your fundamental online freedoms.
