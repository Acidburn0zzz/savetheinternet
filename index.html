What
the
Internet
Needs

1. All data should be treated equally. Enshrining net neutrality is the only way to ensure that the internet will remain an open platform for innovation and a mechanism for the fostering of human rights.

2. Network discrimination must be outlawed: The establishment of a tiered internet must be prevented.

3. No anti competitive agreements: The EU should guarantee an online environment where competition and innovation can thrive.

4. No censorship: Content decisions are made by a competent judicial authority subject to transparency and public oversight - not corporations, who do not have the capacity to be judge, jury, and police.

What
is Going
on in
the EU

Last year, thousand of citizens took action to Save the Internet during the fight for net neutrality in the European Parliament. Supported by this campaign, more than 40,000 faxes, thousands of emails, and hundreds of phone calls were made and together, we convinced politicians to defend our fundamental rights online.

But the fight isn’t over. Even now, the Council of the European Union is debating about whether they should support the Parliament’s strong call for net neutrality - or open the door to a tiered internet, which would be devastating to the long-term welfare of the telecoms market, competition, innovation, and human rights online.
